# Security Policy

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you believe you have found a security vulnerability in this project or in
the site it publishes at **qnap.egeek.tech**, please report it to us
privately.

**Contact:** `security@egeek.tech`

Please **do not** open a public GitHub issue, pull request, or discussion
that contains exploit details, working proof-of-concept, or other
information that could be used against the running site before we have had
the opportunity to investigate and remediate.

### What to include

When reporting, the following helps us triage quickly:

- A clear description of the issue and the affected component or URL
- The steps to reproduce, ideally with curl / browser instructions
- The impact you believe the issue has
- Your name and a way to contact you for follow-up (if you wish credit)

### What to expect

- An acknowledgement within **5 business days** of receipt
- A first assessment (severity, scope, next steps) within **10 business days**
- A coordinated disclosure timeline — typically 60–90 days, longer for
  complex or third-party issues

We will keep you informed throughout the process and will credit you in any
public advisory we publish, unless you ask us not to.

### Scope

- The contents of this repository
- The site hosted at `qnap.egeek.tech`
- The Cloudflare Worker deployed from this repository

Out of scope:

- Reports based on outdated, unsupported, or fork builds
- Issues that require physical access to a developer's machine
- Vulnerabilities in third-party platforms (please report those upstream)
- Findings that depend on browser misconfiguration or extensions

### Safe harbour

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

- Make a good-faith effort to comply with this policy
- Do not access, modify, or exfiltrate data that is not their own
- Do not perform denial-of-service testing against production systems
- Disclose privately and give us reasonable time to remediate

Thank you for helping keep qnap.egeek.tech and its visitors safe.
